Reloading takes a full round with the exception of heavy rifles, which can be loaded and fired in the same round.

 

Moving while attacking

Characters can move and attack (–2 to hit penalty) at the same time if the movement is not too complex (i.e., closing to fight hand to hand, going up stairs, riding a horse, jumping up or down from reasonable heights).

Bonus damage when to hit exceeded

For the amount you exceed your to hit role add a point of damage

Additional Weapon Proficiency

A weapon proficiency may be used to acquire quick draw, the ability to draw and use a weapon in the same combat segment

Weapon Specialization

Any character class can specialize in a particular weapon by designating additional weapon proficiencies to a weapon type. Add +1 to hit for each weapon proficiency added over and above first.

Weapon Proficiency for multi-class characters

If multi-class, weapon proficiency can only be taken in one character class.

Alignment

There is no character Alignment

Character Class Restriction

Most Character restriction including race, weapon, and armor restrictions have been relaxed. However, wearing certain types of armor may interfere with character abilities

Combat Rounds

segment equals one second

10 segment rounds

Turn equals 10 minutes

Note - spell duration measured in rounds may need to be converted to minutes

Initiative Rolls

One attacker per round - 1D10 modified by dexterity adjustment

Two attacks per round - 1st attack - 1D10 modified by dexterity adjustment,

2nd attack - 1d4 (no dex adjustment) added to first attack segment

 

Hit Points

First level hit points equal constitution, no hit point constitution adjustments per level

Big or burly creature (ogres and dwarves) get hit point bonus

Small or light wait creature (hobbits and elves) get hit point reduction

Death occurs when a character reaches negative hit points in excess of their constitution

A character is conscious at negative hit points if they role under their constitution when they took the damage.

Knocked out - a character is knocked out when ever they take hit points in excess of their constitution in the same attack unless they role under their constitution

Clerics

Clerics do not have to memorize spells. They can cast any spell within their ability

Wounds

When a character takes damage in excess of five points, they may have a wound which continues to "bleed" one point a round per wound until healed or bandaged

Usually determined by a con roll but not all damage attacks may leave bleeding wounds (magical attacks, clubs). A character may have multiple wounds bleeding at the same time.
