Game #12 Summary
Play Date:  November 17, 2000
 
Players in Attendance: 
   Mike, Diane, Kevin, & Bryon
 
Dinner:
    Domino's Pizza
 
Game Time Line:
    Start:  June 12th at sunset, flying not far from Arden city
    Stop:  June 15th, afternoon, at the Imperial Palace of Arden
    Elapsed time: 3 days
 
The Party:
    Aspen, Aune, Calelith, and Halse
 
Encounters:
    Arden Airfield:
        Colonel Roberta Scott (mother of Major Raquel Scott)
        Captain Sparks (warrior wizard)
        Secret Service Agent Darrell
    Arden Palace:
        Inspector Moran
        3 Imperial Advisors
        Rick Green
        Senator Green (Rick's father)
        Anna
        Prime Minister    
 
Game Highlights:
 
Arden Airfield
We begin with the party flying along in the commandeered mail plane from Roahock Airfield.  The flight is pleasant, the weather is good, and the fuel is plentiful - something must be wrong!  Fortunately for the party Aspen is a skilled pilot and manages to get them all well out of harms way.  Unfortunately as Aspen nears the supposed location of Arden Airfield he finds himself lost.  The landmarks are unfamiliar and there are no charts in the plane due to the hasty way in which the party departed Roahock.  Fortune once again smiles on the party as Aspen eventually recognizes his uncle's farm.  From there we follow the train tracks in towards the capital.

Soon thereafter the party notices another plane off in the distance.  It closes in and is quickly identified as an imperial fighter.  Aspen nods to the pilot as he nears and there is indication that we should follow them.  We are soon joined by several other planes as escort which help us find our way to the Arden Airfield.

Arden Airfield is absolutely crawling with activity, apparently the imperial military was no longer going to underestimate the power and determination of Maloe to retrieve what was once his from the party.  After landing the party is greeted by Colonel Roberta Scott, mother of Major Scott, Captain Sparks, a wizard warrior who is actually glowing (how rude!), and Secret Service Agent Darrell who is wearing a dark suit and glasses.  Colonel Scott welcomes the party.  Aspen is pleased to find out that that the mail plane they have commandeered is theirs to use as they see fit!
 
To the Palace
The party leaves the airfield in a blue armored car, escorted to the imperial palace by the rest of the dignitaries that welcomed them.  We use the time to catch up on what has happened since we left Roahock.  Apparently Maloe and his cronies left soon after the party flew away, and they haven't been heard of or seen since.  Along the way towards the palace Aune feels a "presence" around her.  She believes it may be an elemental presence of some sort, probably either air or earth.  When the party gets about 40 miles from the palace they reach the outer boundaries of the palace security area and curiously Captain Sparks indicates he must leave at this point. Apparently he is not authorized to enter the palace grounds!
 
Arden Palace
Finally arriving at the Palace proper there are many guards around.  Security is very strong there and Aune once again detects an elemental presence.  This time she believes that there are air elementals flying around the entire palace area.  A butler greets us and prepares to escort us to our rooms. The guest quarters are huge and luxurious, with private baths and regal decor.  Unfortunately the party's gear arrival was delayed when we departed Roahock so we didn't have any appropriate attire to greet the emperor.  Calelith asked about the availability of some more appropriate clothing and as quick as a snap a tailor is dispatched to measure up the entire party and enquire about what sort of clothing we would require.  After much deliberation the party decides on thematic formal clothing for the entourage utilizing a blue and burgundy theme.

Once discharged to their rooms Aune decides to partake in a bubble bath while Calelith and Aspen decide to wander around the palace grounds and chambers.  Calelith makes his rounds covering as much of the buildings as he can, all along making careful mental note about its overall structure and layout.  After a few hours the tailor returns for a second fitting and soon after the party is escorted to the dining hall for dinner.
 
Dinner in the Palace
The party is happy to see Inspector Moran waiting for them at the dinner table.  It's been a while since we've seen him and it's good to catch up on all the happenings that have been going on.  Moran indicates the party has been doing a really super job and that soon they should be reporting back to him again.  The plan at this point is to give the emperor the key and fill them in on everything we have seen and learned.

As everyone enjoys the seafood dinner the party inquires about the marble they had purchased many weeks ago in Black Bay.  Moran seems to be concerned yet amused by the whole thing.  Apparently eventually the marble purchase was funded by a rider that was attached to another bill.  A post office in a small backwards town had developed a leaking roof and other problems and was soliciting funding for its repair.  Now the town's post office is the pride of the county as it is all rebuilt using the fancy marble the party had purchased!

Meeting with the Imperial Advisors
After dinner the party went back to their rooms where their new clothes were waiting for them.  Soon it was time to have an audience with the emperor so we are escorted to a meeting room.  Colonel Scott and Agent Darrell are there along with Moran.  Eventually 3 imperial advisors arrive, clothed in monk-like robes.  The advisors ask the party about all that they have found and ask to see the key.  They tell the party that they have been researching the key and its associated legend since they heard about our findings and will now use the key itself to help finish their work after a few days.  The party is advised that the evil druid Maloe has been using the key and the wand Calelith took from him to identify the parties whereabouts by scrying.  In order to prevent Maloe from finding the party again they must part with these two objects.

Eventually Aune is convinced that the key will be safe with the advisors in the palace and pulls out the key to give to them.  Calelith also agrees to surrender the wand, fortunately the advisors offer to replace it with similar device that holds 20 spell points of charge.  The meeting goes well and the advisors invite the party to stay at the palace for two days until their research is complete and at which time a special ceremony will be held in honor of the party!
 
Hanging out at the Palace
Everyone enjoys the prospect of a little free time to rest and relax in the lavish surroundings of the palace.  Aune decides to study history for a few days and engage in some practice and training with the guards in the barracks.  Halse decides to join her in the military training as well.  Calelith decides to hang out in the shipping and receiving area of the palace, he's very interested in the commerce which is going on there. (?!)  Aspen decides to pretty much just hang out and relax during the respite.

Calelith discovers that the shipping and receiving area does not seem to be anywhere near large enough to accommodate all the supplies that must be necessary to keep the palace running but doesn't figure out what is truly going on there.

Aune is pleasantly surprised to find out that the boxes of chocolate the party acquired from the smugglers is indeed extremely valuable here in Arden.  The tailor indicates that each box has a value of 500 crowns!  Aune decides to trade two boxes of the rare confectionary for a lovely party gown and set of jewelry.
 
Ceremony in honor of the Imperial Marshals of Arden
The day of the ceremony finally arrives, the party finds itself in a big banquet room full of dignitaries and plenty of scrumptious food.  It is there that we once again meet up with Rick Green and hear the familiar "howdy" of Anna, Rick's girlfriend.  We also meet, amongst others, Rick Green's father Senator Green and the Prime Minister herself.  There is much discussion of all the party has gone through and how much appreciated our efforts are.  It's clear the party is considered to be a "rising star" in the eyes of the Arden empire.  Our incident involving the train wreck was in all the newspapers and of course our rescue of the Senator's son has made us real heroes in the eyes of the government and common folk.

Of course, with the party finding itself surrounded by all sorts of powerful political figures there was ample opportunity to inquire about certain controversial government practices and to make "suggestions" about how things could be improved in the empire.  Aune expresses concern about her homeland being annexed while the politicos do their best to spin the action in a positive vein.  Calelith also jumps in to the foray by announcing that he is deeply concerned about the lack of equality for non-human races in the empire.  When will non-humans be considered equals and citizens?  Calelith is not particularly gratified by their proclamation that due to his great deeds he, at least, is now an honorary citizen of the empire!  Aspen finds himself having a great time and finds nothing whatsoever to complain about.

Eventually the Prime Minister snaps her fingers to gather the attendants attention and reads a lengthy proclamation.  In defense of the empire and the senate, due to our great deeds in handling the train wreck incident and in rescuing Rick Green, the party is awarded with the grand Imperial Star.  The party soon after finds out that these are the only Imperial Stars awarded this entire year and that the medals were bestowed upon the party by a unanimous vote in the Senate!  Rising stars indeed!
 
Later in the evening the double doors at the end of the banquet room finally open, the eldest Imperial Advisor greets the party and asks us to follow him down the hallway.  At the end of the hallway we enter another room where we gather around a large desk.  The Advisor extends his gratitude to the party for all we have done and expresses the regrets of the emperor who will unfortunately be unable to meet us.  (Aspen begins to wonder if the emperor really even exists at this point.)  We are informed that they have discovered that Maloe is now intending to construct another key to gain the access he seeks, it is unclear whether he will succeed or not.  The best plan, now, is to take the key we have and go and retrieve whatever part of the relic sword it will unlock.  Only then will it's safety be assured.  The key is presented back to the party in a thick metal box that will prevent it from being located by scrying.  A special magical glyph is put on it so that only the party can unlock it without danger.  A similar form of protection is applied to a small chest containing 50000 GP worth of gems.  We also get a bottle containing four teleportation pills that we can use to instantly bring us back to the palace once our mission is complete.  The Advisor tells us that the entire resources of the the empire are at our disposal to accomplish this great mission.
 
At games end...
The party is in the process of preparing for their journey down to the peninsula of Dartmen where hopefully we will be able to identify and convince a powerful Gnome cleric to bless the key so that we may use it without danger of having our heads bashed in.  We also debate what method of transportation we will use, perhaps a twin engine flying boat may be the best form of transportation for the next leg of this journey...
