Game Highlights:
 
Day 1:
 
Read All About It
While Calelith, Aune, and Turell were in town, they picked up a copy of the Arden Harold.  In one of the headlines, Jack Spear reported that Marshals had saved a train from an Orc attack.  An interview with Edgar had indicated that there was involvement from the military as well.  Travel through this region was expected to be safe in the future as a squadron of Rangers had parachuted in and slaughtered the Orcs.  In other news, there is religious unrest in the South of the empire and the Emperor declared a holiday for the Lost Legion who disappeared over the Sand Sea five years ago.
 
Clean Up and Head Out
Before exiting the Haunted House in search of Rick Green, a little clean up was in order.  The Lizard Men retrieved the 40 rifles and 148 grenades that had been dumped overboard by the smuggler's crew earlier. These were buried in the forest near the mansion for safe keeping.  They found Calelith's rifle as well, which Aune cleaned and made serviceable once again.  The Lizard Men towed a boat with Calelith, Aune, Aspen, Halse, Turell, and Punt to an abandoned fishing village for the rendezvous with the smuggler's ship.  At this time, it was discovered that neither Aune nor Aspen were particularly fond of the sea as they turned green and hung over the side during most of the trip.  Although sea sick, Aspen noticed a light going out in one of the "abandoned" buildings as the village was approached.  Calelith investigated and discovered that there were goblins inside.  Although the party would surely have beaten them in any fair fight, our mission was to rescue Rick.  Taking the signaling lantern to a bluff near the village, Aspen and Calelith signal the ship.  The ship responds and the party heads out for the rendezvous.
 
The Voyage
Upon boarding the Sea Shadow, the party found a mostly middle aged crew numbering a dozen.  The most notable members were Lowar, the young (~23 years old) human female first mate, and Bosum, an old but burly Half Orc and of course, Punt, the Hobbit Thief. The rest of the crew was human men and women wearing studded leather armor.  The Sea Shadow also had five passengers, Keshesssss, a Lizard Men cleric, and four other Lizard Men.  The ship is heavily armed.  There are a multitude of hand held weapons and firearms throughout the ship in easy reach of any crew member.  There are machine guns mounted in the crow's nest on the center mast as well on the aft or fore castles.  A small artillery piece is also positioned on the fore castle.  There are several small barrels connected to tubing which run down to the water line on both sides of the ship (this appears to be Sea Devil repellent similar to what Sabeth was using at the Haunted House).  There are also iron bombs the size of soccer balls mounted on racks on both sides of the ship (these appear to be some kind of depth charge).       
 
Lowar invited the party to her cabin to discuss the voyage.  Bosum, Punt, and Keshesssss also joined in on the discussion.  Lowar first informed the party that they must each be armed and on deck during hours of darkness to repel a Sea Devil attack if necessary.  During the day the party can sleep or explore the ship, but must stay out of locked cabins and not poke around the cargo.  Aspen offers the services of his mechanical expertise during the voyage.  The discussion then moves to what happened to “the boy” (Rick).  Lowar tells the party that the crew will help the party find the boy because they are “business people” and not kidnappers. 
 
Lowar tells the party that the boy is being held at Island Keep, a Zair military outpost on an atoll three days northeast of their current position.  The island is supplied by both air (float plane) and sea (submarine).  On the island there is a small castle with a garrison of about 30.  The castle is commanded by Commandant Schoader.  The boy is being held in one of the three towers of the castle, but they don’t know which.  The island also has a village of 12 workers that support the military operations there, as well as an airfield, and two small ports (one for the military and one for the civilians).  There is also a small church like structure just outside of the village.  The church is a Protestant branch of Aune’s church.  The church houses a priest and his assistant.   In addition, on the other side of the island on top of a bluff, there is a large ancient stonehenge type structure from one of the old world religions.  Some Lizard Men who scouted the island for the crew found a grate under the water near the castle.  The grate seemed to guard a large sewage drain from the castle, but they did not know exactly where the drain pipe went.  Lowar says they will set course for Island Keep but must make a stop to drop off the Lizard Men at a swamp on the coast near the Empire’s border.
 
During the conversation, it becomes clear that Lowar is in command and the party wonders where is the Captain?  Aspen overhears Punt whispering to Lowar that he thinks the party could help them. 
 
The group goes back on deck.  Lowar offers a small cabin for the men to share and the Captain’s cabin for Aune.  Aune invites the party to the Captain’s cabin for conference.  They notice a painting on the wall of what appears to be Lowar’s mother and the party speculates that Lowar is the Captain’s daughter.  In addition next to a very nice desk, there is a framed newspaper article about a party that saved a town from a pack of gnolls.  In the article there is a picture of a younger Bosum and Punt, a cleric, and a man who must be the Captain.  
 
The first day of the voyage is uneventful.  The party spends the time getting to know the crew.  Halse gambles with the crew and does quite well as he wins an old magic users book with three intact spells.  Aspen learns a significant amount about how to maintain the ship’s engines. Keshesssss offers to Aspen that he can get Aspen’s chest wound healed once they get to the Lizard Men lair.  Aspen gratefully accepts.  The party also learns that Punt is the ship's cook and find out he’s pretty good at it.   It is also noted that some of the crew members are married. 
 
Upon gaining acceptance by the crew, Lowar decides to confide in the party and explain why they were helping Sabeth smuggle weapons into the Empire.  The Captain was taken prisoner by Katasa, (Zair’s KGB like organization), the crew was told if they didn’t obey Sabeth’s (a Katasa agent) orders, the Captain would be executed.  By sneaking around the castle, Punt had figured out that the Captain was being held in one of the towers, the same tower that they now believe the boy is being held. 
 
Day 2:
 
The Lizard Men Lair
On the second evening the Sea Shadow reaches the Lizards Men lair.  Aspen, Aune, and Turrell accompany the Lizard Men to their lair.  Upon examining the Lizard Men lair, Aune determines that the structure was actually constructed by gnomes.  The party is taken to meet the Lizard Men chief who thanks them for freeing his people from Sabeth’s grasp.  As a symbol of appreciation he gives each party member a medal that will identify to all Lizard Men that the party members are allies.   He also allows the Lizard Men high priestess to cure Aspen’s chest wound and provides the party with a sea sickness cure.  The chief offers the party a seven hours water breathing potion that can be split into smaller doses but only if it is used to rescue the Captain.  The party agrees.  Aune inquires to the high priestess on the nature of the stonehenge structure on the island.  She says it is a place of worship for the Sea Elves.  Keshesssss decides to accompany the party and crew to Island Keep and brings four Lizard Men warriors who can speak the language of the party.   The Sea Shadow now heads toward Island Keep.  The night is quiet without a Sea Devil attack.
 
Day 3:
 
The Rift
Early on the third day the Sea Shadow crosses the Rift, a barrier of turbulent waters where the warm waters of the southern sea collide with the colder waters of the northern sea.  The ship traverses the Rift with out incidence.  The Sea Devils do not live in the colder waters of the North Sea and are no longer a threat.
 
Island Keep
The Sea Shadow approaches Island Keep at dusk.  The Lizard Men swim to the island to see if the could breach the grate guarding the sewage drain pipe.  They quickly returned saying the grate was severely rusted and easily breached.  The drain tunnel led to a cesspool under the castle and at the top of the cesspool was a man hole cover.  Aspen, Calelith, and Aune decide to go to shore.  Aspen and Calelith will use some of the water breathing potion and be taken up the drain pipe by the Lizard Men to “scout out the castle”.  Aune will investigate the island on foot.  One of the spells in Halse’s newly acquired spell book is invisibility.  Halse only has enough spell power to cast it once and it is used to make Aspen invisible.  
 
Aspen and Calelith slide open the man hole cover and begin to scout out the castle, leaving the Lizard Men at the opening.  They investigate the closest tower.  Aspen listens at the door and hears voices.  While Calelith hides, Aspen looks inside and sees a couple of solders and what appears to be soldiers' quarters.   Deciding not to attempt to enter the tower here, Calelith climbs the outside stairs to the top of the battlements to the second story of the tower, and then climbs the out side of the tower to the balcony on the third floor.  He enters the room and quickly determines that he is in the Commandant’s quarters.  He notices a very, very nice long sword mounted on the wall but refrains from taking it.  He goes down to the second floor in the tower and finds more officers' quarters.  He heads back up stairs and climbs back down on the outside. 
 
Aspen goes to check out the next tower.  The door to this tower is on the battlement and goes to the tower's second floor.  The door is locked and Aspen is unable to pick it.  He goes and gets Calelith from his hiding place.  Unfortunately, while sneaking to the tower, Calelith is seen by a couple a civilian workers moving crates on the castle grounds.  When the workers ask who he is, Calelith tells them he’s the “beer guy”.  The workers buy it and enthusiastically ask Calelith to bring them some.   However, the gate guard hearing the commotion comes to investigate and Calelith quickly eludes the guard by jumping off the wall. Calelith works his way back to the tower door and successfully picks the lock.  Calelith and Aspen enter the room.  The guard sees Calelith but before he can react, Calelith casts a spell and puts the guard to sleep.  He then ties the guard up.
 
Aspen works his way down to the first floor of the tower.  Still invisible, he easily sneaks past the two guards he finds there and looks into the cell they are guarding.  He sees an older bearded man (the Captain) and Rick!  They have finally found him.   He returns to Calelith who comes down the stairs and cast a color spray spell.  Unfortunately he only knocks out one of the guards.  The other pulls his gun and charges Calelith.  Calelith retreats slightly, pulls his sword and misses the guard.  The guard shoots Calelith, wounding him, then grabs him.  Meanwhile, Aspen unlocks the cell and gives the Captain a sword and gun.  The Captain charges the guard giving Calelith the chance to break free.
 
Meanwhile, Aune has made her way to the church structure.  She enters the structure and finds what looks more like a small archeological museum than a church.  There are archeological artifacts and related text all over.   In the middle of the room are the priest and his assistant discussing an object on a table.  They are oblivious to Aune’s approach.   Aune announces her presence and startles the two men.  The priest quickly figures out Aune’s holy nature and though he has no idea how she got there or what she wants, he completely accepts her into his confidence.  He explains that he and his assistant are investigating the ancient stonehenge.  Aune senses a low level of divine energy from many of the artifacts, with the largest amount from small statue of an ancient sea god.  She can also sense the stone hinge.  Indeed, it feels that the stone hinge is actually trying to repel her.  The priest says that he believes that the monument is a source of great power and that they have discovered what they think is a door to chambers beneath the structure.  Since Aune has experience with ancient cultures, he invites her to participate in the “dig”.   He tells her that a team of archeologists should be arriving by plane the next day.  Aune takes a couple of texts to study and leaves, heading for the castle.

At Game Conclusion
Aspen, who is still invisible, and Calelith have found and freed Rick and the Captain.  However, Calelith has cast most of his spells and is wounded and they are in the middle of a fight with the remaining guard.  They are also still in the castle who’s occupants are at some level aware of their presence.  Aune is nearby but outside the castle.  The four Lizard Men are waiting in the cesspool.  Halse, Turell, and the rest of the crew of the crew are anxiously waiting offshore on the Sea Shadow.
