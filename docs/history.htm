The 1919 D&D Game

World History Draft 11/08/99

 

The Old World

For thousands of years, three great races, elves, orcs, and dwarves, dominated these great lands. From time to time a great leader would unite the various tribes of one of the races and their influence and power would eclipse the others. However, this dominance was fleeting and was always diminished by war, death, and corruption. Their were many other races, goblins, gnomes, ogres, gnolls, and hobbits. These minor races were scattered through out the lands, but never in numbers that could challenge the greater races. Thus the world changed very little from one century to the next. Then everything changed. A new race arrived. A tribe of nomads wandered out of the Ice Desert. Not elf, orc, dwarf, or any of the minor races. It was a new race that combined the best characteristics of each of the great races. They had the intelligence of the elves, the ingenuity of the Dwarves, and the strength of the orcs. In the beginning, the new race was known as the ice people. But over time, they came to be called… human.

The Ice People

Where the Ice People came from and why they came the great lands have long been forgotten. The dwarves of the Northern Range were the first to encounter them. The dwarves were enthusiastic to establish trade with surface dwellers and quickly established a harmonious relationship with the newcomers. The dwarves found the humans to be eager learners and they taught them metal working and mining skills.

Eventually as more humans arrived and their population grew, the Ice people enter the low lands. Once in the low lands, they were captured and enslaved by various orc tribes. Because the orcs were almost always at war with the other races and other orc tribes, humans quickly learned a great deal about various weapons and types of warfare.

The Orc Wars and the Alliance

Under the great orc leader, Rocar the Mighty, the orc tribes united and fought a fierce war with the elves and dwarves. In order to defeat the combined orc armies, the dwarves and elves encouraged the enslaved humans to rebel against their orc master by secretly providing them with weapons and training.

The human-dwarf-elf alliance proved a formidable adversary to the orcs, but not an invincible one. The orc wars raged for years. One of the great advantages of the alliance was the ability of the elves to use magic against the orcs. But there were not enough elven wizards to support the war effort. In order to increase the pool of magic users, the elves began to train humans in the arts of wizardry.

Though the alliance was powerful, the united orc tribes under the forceful rule of Rocar proved too powerful and continued to conquer elven and dwarven lands. All seemed lost until a small group of humans, dressed as slaves, infiltrated Rocar’s war camp and in suicide attack, killed Rocar. Though none of the humans in the attacked survived, the effect was devastating on the orcs. With Rocar gone, the orcs began to fight among themselves in ensuing power vacuum. In complete disarray, the orcs no longer posed a credible threat. In a matter of months, the alliance forced most of the orcs from the low lands. Then, ironically, the humans who were once slaves themselves enslaved their former orc masters.

The Rise of the Humans

With the skills they learned from their allies and enemies and their ever-increasing numbers, humans quickly joined the ranks of the great races.

Humans then began to develop their own skills. The humans developed their own calendar with the first year beginning when the first nomadic tribe discovered the great lands.

As the human population grew, they spread through out the lands. However, with no significant external enemies, the various human tribes began to war among themselves. These human wars occasionally involved their former allies, the dwarves and elves.

The elves became alarmed by the unchecked growth of the human race. The elves saw inter-human warfare as a vehicle to keep humans from becoming too powerful. To this end, the elves frequently supported the losing human tribes to perpetuate the wars.

However, even given the inter-tribe wars, humans still flourished and advanced.

Humans develop ships and navigation and began to sail the inner seas at to nearby islands in the great west ocean. However, extensive exploration of the great west ocean was prevented by the presents of "Sea Devils" (an aggressive aquatic race) that attacked any ship on the open seas at night. All expeditions attempting to cross the Great West Sea are lost

Human made significant advances in medical science which reduced human death rates and lengthened their life span.

Human kingdom with feudal monarchies rose to power.

Humans mastered wizardry, even exceeding the abilities of the elves who had taught them.

Year 1125, The First Science Master, Pentock (The first of a series of great scientists who greatly contributed to human technological advancements), established the first human university which she called The Academy of Kingdoms. "We will learn to do everything done with magic today - without it tomorrow."

Year 1250 Gun Powder discovered.

With the development of firearms and cannons, the human wars intensify and evolve all of the human kingdoms. Human monarchies frequently use peasant human subjects (as well as orc slaves) as cannon fodder in the wars resulting in the death of thousands of human peasants each year.

Trade between the human kingdoms and the other races expands and a human merchant class of educated and ambitious non-nobles develops. The merchant class begins to resent the inaccessibility of the privileged classes.

1710 Dawn of an empire

A large group from the merchant class form the Peoples Political Party (PPP) which declares all humans as equals and demand an end to the class structure that separate the nobility from peasantry. The PPP is lead by a charismatic and tireless leader, Ven Tiran.

The King of the oldest and largest human kingdom tries to crush the PPP but a rebellion ensues and the king and his government are overthrown. The PPP establishes a human republic. A senate is established with members elected by citizens. Citizenship is granted to any human over 15 who "proves" their worth to the republic. ("worth" could be proved in any number of ways including through military services, art, business, or science) All citizens are granted rights of protection. The senate quickly imposes military conscription. This creates an enormous army, which are then well trained.

1725

Ven Tiran rises in power in the republic and is declared Prime Minister and chief of the military.

Ven Tiran quickly crushes several surrounding kingdoms.

As the republic grows, it was divided into states and territories.

The Elves

There are three major elven races, the High, Wood, and Grey Elves.

The High Elves are the most common member of the elven race. They are tall, fair skinned, and have light colored hair. They are a civilized, if not arrogant, forest dwelling race. They are highly educated and capable a producing some of the finest metal and art work. Wizards and Clerics are not uncommon among their elite. They are a formidable fighting force when threatened. Though there are several independent tribes, they all share a common queen who has supreme sovereignty in times of crisis.

The next most common elven race is the Wood Elves. The Wood Elves are a more primitive and reclusive cousin of the High Elves. Their skin and hair are darker in color and they are also somewhat shorter than other elves. They live primarily in tree dwellings, deep in the forests. They are excellent trapper and hunters, but they are not nearly as well educated. They also don’t produce fineries that can compare with those made by the High Elves. Because of these differences, the High Elves frequently treat their Wood Elves cousins as an unworthy addition to the elven race and do not include Wood Elves in any of their activities. This has bread discontent between the two races.

The most rare, but most powerful, race of elves are the Grey Elves. Many of the greatest wizards of all time have been Grey Elves. They are a highly magical race capable of unimaginable powers. Few have ever seen them. Very little is known of them, but they are feared and respected by all other elven and non-elven races.

Elven Wars - 1899

Elves disdain the new human technologies and refuse to adopt the advances. Imperial industrialization begins to pollute lands. Loggers begin large-scale clear cutting of forest. The elves become very upset at the rate of destruction of natural resources. The High Elves try and negotiate limits to imperial consumption. Negotiation fail and elven-Imperial relation’s cool.

Disputes arise on the border of the elven woods. Elves begin to sabotage Imperial mining and timber cutting equipment. A group of radical elves capture a coal power electrical plant and sabotage it. When Imperial authorities storm the plant, the elves are gone and many human plant workers are found dead. The Imperial Senate declares war on the elves and request the Emperor to send in troops. The Emperor delegates the war to his generals.

The elves are found to be nearly invincible in the woods. Using guerrilla tactics, the Elven tribes defeat Imperial forces battle after battle, but the imperial leaders are persistent and continue to assaults elven enclaves. Imperial casualties are significant and the Senate blames the generals for mismanaging the war.

The Final Solution

1904, the Emperor appoints Colonel Westscott to take command of the Elven campaign. He develops a three part final solution to the elven problem.

  1. Establish an elite military force trained in forest warfare to fight the elves - Rangers.
  2. Systematically burn elven forest.
  3. Break elven alliance by secretly negotiating with the Wood Elves, to betray their arrogant elven cousins, the High and Gray Elves.

Though ineffectual at first, the Imperial Rangers begin to wage successful attacks on elven strongholds. Millions of acres of elven forest are burned. Colonel Westscott personally negotiated a secret treaty with the Wood Elves guaranteeing them large portions of forest free of Imperial incursions. The wood elves treaty also designates large portions of woods that were currently in the domain of High Elf tribes. In exchange, the Wood Elves assist Imperial forces in tracking down and eliminating High Elf resistance. However, the Wood Elves would not agreed to help the empire against the Grey Elves.

1908, faced with the betrayal by their elven cousins, destruction of their homeland, and mounting casualties, the High Elf queen commands the High Elves within the empire to capitulate. A few High Elf tribes refuse to quit fighting and are forced to flee the Empire. The remaining High Elf tribes are resettled on reservations. The elven reservations are allowed independence and are not subject to imperial rule.

Imperial forces are unable to locate and engage Grey Elf forces or settlements. Colonel Westscott reports to the Emperor and Senate that without the alliance of the High and Wood Elves, the Grey Elves are no longer a significant threat to the Empire.

1909, The Elven Wars are officially ended.

The League of Realms offers sanctuary to any elves leaving the empire. Several High Elf tribes ally with the League and join in their war against the empire.

Prejudice towards elves is significant throughout the empire.

Imperial States and Territories have occasionally violated elven sovereignty and treaties. In many of these instances, imperial authorities have intervened on the elves behalf to thwart State and Territory actions.

 

 

 

Orcs and Dorks

Bands of orcs in the outer territories and outside the empire are well organized and even sea barring. They continue to be a threat to those they encounter.

Though thought of as a lower class, domesticated orcs have been fully assimilated into the empire. Many can read and are frequently employed in manual labor positions in farming and construction. The domesticated orcs are commonly referred to as Dorks to distinguish them from their uncivilized brethren. Many Dorks have joined the Imperial Armies and a few have even been admitted to schools of higher education. The Dorks have their own religion and Dork churches are not uncommon. Though uncommon, a few States and Territories are allowing Dorks to become citizens.

Dwarves and Gnomes

Dwarves and humans continue trading. Dwarven craftsmanship is considered the finest. Imperial Territories bordering the Dwarven Mountains allow dwarves to become citizens. Dwarves are honored and treated with a great deal of respect in most parts of the empire.

Gnomes are rare but have learned to combine magic and technology like no other race.

Currency

The empire has issued the Imperial Crown as the official currency. Exchanges using platinum, gold, and silver pieces have been banned within imperial boarders. However, the ban is not enforced in the outer territories where both currencies are widely used.

Year 1919

The Empire is struggling. Emperor Talon is very old and is rarely heard from. The Trench War with the United Realms and Free Elves in the Northwest has stagnated. Orc raiding and pirating had become a serious problem. There is religious unrest in Southern Territories and minor rebellions in the Eastern Territories. A plague rages in the South East. Taxes, the draft, and war casualties are causing social unrest throughout the empire. Hunger, disease, and poverty plague many of the major cities. Corruption is rampant.

Political reforms and non-citizen rights legislation is currently being debated in the Imperial Senate.
