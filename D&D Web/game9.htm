Game Highlights:
 
Day 1:
 
Escape from Island Keep
The fight in the tower continues.  The invisible Aspen tells Rick to tie up the unconscious guard while Calelith and the Captain battle the remaining guard.  Rick says he can’t see Aspen, but he can sure smell him.  Calelith cast a shield spell.  The guard gets off one more shot but misses the captain.  The captain promptly disarms the guard and puts the point of his sword to the guards chest and a points his gun at the guard’s head.  The Captain asked the guard to choose his death.  The guard promptly surrenders and Rick ties him up.
 
Taking advantage of his continuing invisibility, Aspen leaves the tower to scout the escape route.  In addition to the two workers moving creates, three Zair soldiers appear to be investigating the area around the cesspool thus cutting off the planned escape rout.  The group decide to use a rope and climb down the wall and make a run for it. Calelith goes first and is promptly seen by the workmen who call out “beer man!” and approach. Calelith quickly grabed a couple of beer bottles that he spotted in the tower and walked down the steps to greet the workmen.  One guards heard the workmen and began to comes over to investigate. Calelith gave the workmen the bottles and said he has to go back and get more.  While this going on, Aspen, Rick, and the Captain climbed down the wall. Calelith ran back up the steps and climbed down to join the other three.  Aune, who is just outside the castle, saw the others climbing down the wall and moved to join them.  Once together, they all made a run for the nearby bushes.   The investigating guard reached the top of the wall, saw the Party running away and yelled for them to stop as he fired a couple of warning shots. Calelith made himself the primary target by placing himself at the back of the flee Party.  One guard managed to get off a lucky shot an Calelith was hit again.  A siren went off alerting the rest of the soldiers on the Island.
 
The Party managed to get out of sight in the bushes.  They couldn’t go directly to the boat without being seen and guards were approaching from the village and the castle.  The Party decided to split up. Calelith, Aune, Rick, and the Captain would climb down the closest cliff to water line.  Aspen, still invisible, would go directly to the boat and get the lizard men to move it to the rest of the Party.  Aspen headed out while the rest of the party climbed down the cliff.  A couple of guards noticed the party down the cliff and summoned assistance then started climbing down.  Seeing the commotion, the Lizard men moved the boat to the party before Aspen had reached them.  The lizard men and the boat reached the Party.  Calelith, Aune, Rick, and the Captain climb in but Aspen couldn’t get there in time.  The guards took several shots at the boat as it proceeded out to see.  Only Aune took a minor hit.  Aspen decided to take a water breathing position and try and swim for it.   As soon as the Party members in the boat reached the Sea Shadow, the Captain ordered it to get under way.  Aspen was still swimming quite far away and began to yell for help.  Fortunately for Aspen, Calelith was listening for Aspen and heard him yelling. Calelith sent the lizard men back to get him.  Aspen and the lizard men barley got back in time to board the ship before it got under way.  Though Island Keep sent planes and a submarine to look for the party, the Sea Shadow managed to allude them.
 
Day 2-5:
 
Back to Black Bay
A day later, the Party and Crew concluded that they have escaped and are no longer in danger from the Realm forces on Island Keep and they have a big party.  The Sea Shadow crosses the Rift but sees no sign of Sea Devils.  The lizard men are dropped off at their lair and the Sea Shadow continues to the Haunted Mansion so the Party can recover their buried weapons.   Aune rows the Party ashore and they discover Border Patrol agents investigation the at the haunted mansion.  However, there is little for them to find because Halse has already had all the evidence and contraband shipped to Black Bay.  The Border Patrol is annoyed that the Marshals shut down the smuggling operation and is here now to reestablish their jurisdiction over the mater.  The Party dug up the buried weapons and transported them to the Sea Shadow without the knowledge of the Border Patrol Agents.  The Sea Shadow then dropped the party off outside of Black Bay.
 
Mission accomplished
On arriving in Black Bay, Calelith reports in to Inspector Moran.  Moran congratulates the party for accomplishing their mission and tells them to take Rick to Eddington and turn him over to Agent Zachery of the Secret Service.  Then he tells the party to report to the Arden Academy branch campus in Eddington for training.  He also tells them not to tell any one else about the Sea Shadow.
 
The Party decides to finalize their deal with Black Bay’s marble quarry and buy several marble columns.  They charge the columns to the opposition party.  (Later that month, Rick’s father and the opposition Federalist Party in the Imperial Senate attach an amendment to an emergency military appropriations bill to purchase marble columns for an old wood post office in South Arden).   
 
The town of Black Bay throws a party for the Party.  Before leaving for Eddington, Turell says goodbye.  He’s not a city guy and this is his home.  He tells the party that he will be living in the abandoned village of his tribe.
 
The Party travels to Eddington and turn Rick over to Agent Zachery.  Rick thanks them for rescuing him and that he hopes to meet them again.  The Party begins training at the Arden Academy.  Aune also trains at Eddington Cathedral and researches the Island Keep stonehenge at Eddington University.
 
Day 8:
An invitation
The Party receives an invitation from Ned Flanders, the Syndicate auditor they had arrested, to dinner at the finest steak house in Eddington.  The party goes to the dinner.  When the Party arrives, they are escorted to a buffet in the VIP banquet hall.  Other diners at the restaurant look and wonder who the honored guests are.  Ned greets the party in the banquet hall.  Aspen says Ned should be wearing horizontal stripes.  Ned says “but I can be so much more productive out here”.  Ned says the party saved his life.  Ned’s “company” had suspicions about Sabeth’s operation and sent Ned to investigate.  Ned is convinced that had he discover the weapons smuggling, as he believes he would have, Sabeth would have had no choice but to him.  Fortunately the Party killed Sabeth first, thereby saving Ned’s life and relieving the need for the Syndicate from taking its own action against Sabeth.  Ned explains that he and his “company” owe the party and he says “we pay our debts”.  Each party member gets a choice of one of an assortment of adventuring items that Ned brought or a party member can call in a “favor” from the Syndicate at some future date.   Each party member decided to take an adventuring item.
 
At the conclusion of the dinner, Ned gives each party member his card “Ned Flanders, Chief Auditor, Bank of Credit and Commerce International”.  Showing this card to Syndicate members indicates that the party member is a “friend”.
 
Day 10:
 
Drafted
Calelith gets a call from Inspector Moran.  Moran tells Calelith that Colonel Brennen has used an obscure reserve activation clause in the Imperial Code to draft the Party into a military operation.  The Colonel has ordered the party to immediately report to a Major Raquel Scott at the Eddington airfield and be prepared to be flown to the front lines.   Moran is willing to “stone wall” the Colonel if the party does not want to go.  Though Calelith has reservation about fighting for the Empire, the party decides to hear the Colonel out and go to the front.  Moran asked if the party wants to have Halse transferred to their unit.  The Party decides that Halse is a asset and agree.  Moran tells then not to risk their lives in combat.  He didn’t spend all this time putting together an elite unit just to have them killed in someone else’s military operation.  In anticipation of flight, Calelith memorizes all feather fall spells. The party reports to the airfield.  They are met by Major Scott.  She tells them to board an amphibious airplane.  They take off and fly to the front. 
 
Colonel Brennen, Black Force, and the Rodenberg Mission
Rodenberg is a Realm city fifty miles behind the front lines.  The city is walled and has been used as a major supply depot and troop recovery center for the Realm armies.  Two weeks ago, the Empire began a major offensive on the Front.  In an attempt to slow the advance of Imperial forces, Realm Strategic Command (RSC) sent much of Rodenberg’s garrison to reinforce overwhelmed Realm troops on the front.   The Imperial General’s received intelligence that Rodenberg was vulnerable and sent an airborne assault team, Black Force, across enemy lines to capture the city.   Black Force is an elite strike and demolition unit.  They have been specially trained to inflict an enormous amount of damage on men, machines, and buildings.  Unfortunately, in prior missions, Black Force’s brutality has inflicted unacceptable collateral damage to civilian populations.  In an attempt to restrain Black Force’s “enthusiasm” and minimize civilian casualties, Imperial Generals put Colonel Brennen, a ten year veteran of the Elven Wars, in command of Black Force for this mission.   The city was fairly close to the front and it was not expected that it would contain very many civilians. 
 
Black Force’s initial attack caught Rodenberg’s garrison by surprise, Back Force was able to quickly capture several key strategic sites in the city.  But the garrison quickly organized and counter attack with savage desperation.  Intense firefights erupted throughout the city.  To Colonel Brennen’s surprise, Rodenberg still maintained a significant civilian population of more than a thousand.  Caught in the cross fire, hundreds of civilians were wounded or killed.  Colonel Brennen hastily organized a number of cease fires to allow the civilian population to take shelter in Rodenberg’s cathedral.  The Holy Church’s influence extends across both the Empire and the Realms.   Imperial and Realm forces each consider the grounds and buildings of the Holy Church as a sanctuary not to be violated.   Though they fought heroically, the undermanned garrison was no match for Black Force and after fifteen hours of intense fighting, Black Force had completely defeated the garrison and controlled the city.  Only a few members of the garrison survived, either by hiding in basements or by taking cover with the civilians in the cathedral.
 
Now in total control of the city, Colonel Brennen turned his attention to phase two of the operation.  There were two choices.  If the Imperial ground offensive was going well, Black Force was to fortify the city and hold out until the main Imperial Army battled its way to them.  If the Imperial offensive got bogged down and if it was unlikely that the Army would reached the city before Realm forces could mount a counter attack capable of retaking the city, then Colonel Brennen had orders to destroy the city and retreat.  This would be tricky because if the main offensive bogged down then it would be difficult for the Imperial forces to maintain air superiority over Rodenberg.  Without air superiority, Black Force would be cut off from supplies and would not be able to be airlifted out if they needed to retreat. 
 
After holding the city for two days, Colonel Brennen received word from the front.  Though the Imperial Army was advancing, their progress was slow.  Also, Imperial air power was suffering an attrition rate significantly greater than that of the Realm Sky Forces.   It was clear to the Colonel that Black Force would not be able to hold the city long enough to be relieved by the Imperial Army nor could he count on Imperial air superiority for long.  He ordered the destruction of the city and began preparations for the retreat.  The fist structures to be destroyed were the extensive ammunition dumps throughout the city.  The dumps were destroyed using incinerating explosives.  The destruction of the dumps had the deadly side effect of releasing significant amounts of poisonous gas from stock piles of chemical weapons.  This made the air in most of Rodenberg toxic.   This had little impact on Black Force.  They were specialist in chemical warfare and possessed the necessary equipment and gas masks to protect themselves.  The civilian refugees in the cathedral were also protected because they had possessed the insight to seal the Cathedral from outside gases using cloth and duct tape.  However, a third unknown occupant of the city was not so fortunate. 
 
Elves in Rodenberg
Not long after the destruction of the first chemical weapons stockpile and poisonous gas began to fill the air.  A member of Black Force made a discovery.  Among the dead left over from the initial assault, new bodies began to appear in the streets, elven bodies.  Clearly the elves had not been in the city during the initial attack, so where did they come from?  Three elven bodies were found and taken to Rodenberg’s administration building where Colonel Brennen had set up his command headquarters.  The Colonel called on the expertise of an Imperial Wizard Warrior, Captain Cane, who was assisting in the Rodenberg operation.   The Captain determined that the elves had only recently died, most likely from poisonous gas.  It also appeared from traces of residual magic radiating from the bodies that they had been hidden from detection using invisibility spells.  If it had not been for the poisonous gas, it is unlikely that Black Force would have ever known these elves were ever in the city.   But even more notable is that these were no “ordinary” elves.  Two of the elves were High Elves.  A typical breed of elves which were currently allied with the Realms and fighting the Empire.  But the third Elf was very unique.  It was a Grey Elf.  The most elite and rarest of elves.  The Colonel had fought elves for ten years.  He has seen thousands of them including the High Elf Queen.  But he had never seen a Grey Elf.  He had nearly come to the conclusion that the all mighty Grey Elves were just a myth.   But here one lay.  But why?  Why would a Grey Elf enter an occupied city in the middle of a war.  Captain Cane went to investigate the area where the bodies had been found.  The Colonel contacted the Military Intelligence Network (MIN) to determine if Realm forces were aware of an elven infiltration of Rodenberg.   When Captain Cane returned, he informed the Colonel that he had found evidence that these elves were not alone.  At least three sets of “foreign” tracks had been discovered.  The tracks were recent and lead to the Cathedral.  MIN had reported that they did not believe that the Realm leadership was aware of the Elves or knew why they were there.  Though MIN’s opinion was far from definitive, this time Colonel Brennen believed their conclusion.
 
Drafting the Marshals
The Rodenberg mission had suddenly become much more complicated.  The Colonel wasn’t going to simply ignore the elves and complete the destruction of the city as planned.  He needed to know what the elves were up to.  Why were they here?  Why were they going to the Cathedral?  He knew it had to be important, possibly more important than his mission to Rodenberg.   This mystery needed to be resolved and quickly.  It was only a matter of time before Black Force would have no choice but to retreat.  He needed help.  Though Captain Cane was a talented wizard and a competent investigator, the Colonel did not believe he held the skills necessary to solve this mystery.  Nor did the Colonel trust any members of Black Force to investigate this matter.  He was also unwilling to violate the sanctuary of the Cathedral.   He needed help. This mystery was going to require a team with diverse skills who could investigate the Cathedral delicately.   The team did not have to be military personnel.  In fact it would be better if they weren’t.   But the team would have to be trustworthy and able to put the Empires interest above there own.   A team of…….Marshals!  Yes, of course, thought the Colonel.  The marshals from the train.   An elf wizard, a paladin, and a ranger.  Who better to investigate this mystery.  But he’d have to act quickly.  He put his aid, Major Raquel Scott, to work on it.  Within a few hours she had located the Marshals, found the legal means to “enlist” their aid, arranged transport, and notified the Marshals superiors, instructing them to order the Marshals to the airstrip in Eddington.  A city only a few hours away by air.
 
Day 11:
The Marshals Arrive in Rodenberg
During the flight to Rodenberg, Major Scott distributed gas mask to the party.  She also gave them tunic marking them as Imperial forces and had them wear parachutes during the flight in case they were shot down.  She explained Black Forces mission to Rodenberg.  She also warned Aspen to be mindful because there was a great deal of bad blood between the Black Force and the Imperial Rangers.  The party saw intense fighting at the front line as they passed over.  They also found it easy to spot Rodenberg.  The city was nearly totally engulfed in flames.  Only the city walls and cathedral appeared intact.   The plane landed on a river that passed through the city.  They docked at the only pier not burning.  As they exited the plane they noticed a soldier in an unmarked black uniform with a flame thrower and fuel tanks strapped to his back.  The soldier walk to a building, shot a jet of flames into it, and did not even pause to watch it explode into flames before he was on to his next target.
 
Major Scott escorted the Party to Colonel Brennen’s operation headquarters.  When the Party entered the colonel’s office, they found him arguing with a Black Force officer.  Both men were wearing synthetic full body armor, the colonel’s Imperial Army Green clashing with the plain black uniform of the Black Force officer.  The argument ended with the colonel yelling “Just do it!” and the Black Force officer barged out.  The Colonel turned around and see the Party and smiled for the first time in weeks.  “Finally, real people to talk to,” he shouted.  The Party greeted the Colonel and introduced their newest member, Halse.  The Colonel apologizes to the party for the carnage they’ve seen.  “You are not seeing the Empire at its best, but it is necessary” he says. The Colonel explained the situation, introduced Captain Cane, and took the Party to see the dead elves.  Upon examining the Grey Elf, Aune determined that he was relatively young and a cleric of a radical religious sect within the Grey Elf community.  Calelith determined that one of the High Elves appeared to be a fighter/wizard but was unable to locate his spell book. 
 
A discussion ensued about the best way the party could gain admittance to the cathedral.  The Colonel mentioned that the refugees in the cathedral were probably in pretty desperate shape and Aune suggested taking water, food, and supplies to them.  “We’ll be the Red Cross bringing aid” Aune explained.   They all agreed on this approach and the Colonel put Major Scott in charge of assembling the supplies for the refugees.  A pile of medical supplies, a barrel off water, and a stack of food rations were collected and put into a hand cart. Calelith decided he would go unarmed pushing the cart.  Aspen kept his sword and bow.  Aune and Halse decided on minimum armaments hidden in their clothing.  Major Scott escorted the party to the Cathedral. 
 
The Cathedral
As the Party approached the outer walls of the cathedral, Major Scott decided to wait in a guard tower at the entrance while the Party tried to make its way inside.  The Party approached the great doors of the cathedral and knocked.  After some time, someone looked out a nearby window.  The party explained they were the Red Cross bringing supplies.  After a few minutes the doors opened and out look a rather haggard looking priest.  Upon recognizing Aune as a paladin, introduced himself as Father Yohan and invited the Party in to the chagrin of three garrison soldiers who had also taken refuge in the cathedral. 
 
The situation in the Cathedral was desperate.  There was no running water or sanitation and the air reached.  Hundreds lay dying from wounds and thirst.  Aspen administered what first aid he could.  Halse and Calelith distributed the small amount of supplies they had brought.  Aune cast every spell she had attempting to save those lives she could.  After all this the Party saw that they had only made a small dent in the suffering of these people.  The Party left to acquire additional supplies.  Major Scott was able to gather up some more water and a few more rations, but was unable to come up with more medical supplies other than a few clean sheets.  The Party took the second set of supplies back to the Cathedral.  Upon this trip, the Party decided to ask Father Yohan what he know.  Father Yohan knew nothing about the elves.  He took the Party on a tour of the Cathedral showing them briefly the central alter and the catacombs underneath.  The catacombs were currently being used to store raw sewage and dead bodies and were thus not very inviting.
 
The Gnome Altar
During the tour the Party  also notice a small wing of the Cathedral that was fenced off.   Aune sensed a unusual holy aura from the area.  Father Yohan explained that prior to Rodenberg being settled, a band of gnomes lived in a small city here.  However, the city was abandoned by the time the first humans settled in the area.   The Holy Church decided to build this cathedral on the site of an ancient crumbling Gnome temple.   The builders tore down the gnome temple, but out of respect to the gnome religion, they left the temple alter intact and in place.  The cathedral was literally built around the gnome alter.  Because Gnomes are a subterranean race, a large underground portion of the Gnome temple beneath the alter was also left undisturbed.  
 
The party asked if they could see the alter up close.  Father Yohan took out an old key and unlocked the gate protecting the alter and the Party went in to take a look.  Aspen and Calelith check the alter for traps and secret doors.  While Aune attempted to translate the ancient writings on the alter.  Though no traps or secret doors were noticed, Aspen and Calelith quickly noticed some obvious scrape marks on the floor on one side of the alter.  The scrapes had recently been made.  From damage that could be seen at the bottom of the alter on the same side, it appeared that the alter had been slid to one side and then slid back.  Aspen check for tracks and found a set of three leading to one side of the alter and then just disappeared.   Aune did not find any special meaning in the ancient writing on the alter and decided to try and pushed on the side of the alter to see if it could be easily moved, but it didn’t budge. Calelith checked to see how the three garrison soldiers were reacting to the Parties inspection of the alter, though it is clear that the soldiers were watching them (along with a hundred refugees), they did not appear to be to particularly agitated or concerned at the Parties current location.   Aspen again looked for some secret handle or place to put some kind of key or offering that could reveal a hidden entrance.  But besides a small dusty stone bowl on the alter, nothing revealed itself.  
 
The Party asked Father Yohan if he had seen any activity around the alter.  He said he hadn’t but he did not spend much time in this area.  He suggested asking one of the refugees who has settled nearby.  The party asked the women refugee nearest the alter.  She said a couple of days earlier (she is not sure if it was one or two days) she noticed the alter area go completely dark.  She could not see anything inside the gate nor did she hear anything unusual.  Calelith surmised that the intruders used a darkness and silence spell to cover their activity.

At Game Conclusion
The Party members are in Rodenberg Cathedral standing next to the ancient Gnome alter.  They can not find an obvious way to proceed with investigation.  All Party members are healthy and at full hit point.  However, they have been up for more than a day and are quite tired.  Aune has used up all her cleric and paladin spell powers healing the refugees.  Calelith still only has feather fall spell memorized.  Outside of the Cathedral, Rodenberg is being destroyed by Black Force and outside Rodenberg, a huge battle is taking place between Imperial forces and the armies of the Realms.  Colonel Brennen and Black Force can not hold Rodenberg much longer so time is short.
